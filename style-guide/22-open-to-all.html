Open to All

For a lot of families, the first question is not about the sport. It is “can we afford this?” This direction answers it first. The front door is built around what it costs to play and how DiscNW keeps it open to everyone: a plain sliding scale, financial aid, and one simple promise — no one is turned away for lack of money.

02 · The idea

Answer the money question first

Most league sites bury the price. You click through three pages before you learn what a season costs, and by then a worried parent has already talked themselves out of it. This direction puts the answer up front. DiscNW uses a sliding scale. There is a base rate that covers the real cost of running a program. If you have more, you can pay a little more and help cover someone else. If money is tight, you pay less. Fees start at $33, financial aid is there if you need it, and no one is turned away. You pick the rate that fits your family. We do not ask for proof.

The point is not to look generous. It is to remove a barrier. A family that can see the real cost, and see that the low rate is offered plainly and without a catch, is a family that shows up.

03 · Palette

Warm, plain, and easy to trust

The colors are warm and quiet, so the page feels like a welcome and not a sales pitch. A cream ground and espresso text do the reading. DiscNW copper is the one bright accent. The three rate tiers each get a calm color so a family can tell them apart at a glance. Photographs keep their own color and are never tinted.

04 · Typography

Clear to read, warm to look at

Two typefaces, both from Google Fonts. Fraunces is a soft serif that gives headlines and prices a human, settled feel. Public Sans carries the body and the small print, plain and legible. There is no machine-style monospace here on purpose. Prices should feel like a person telling you the number, not a receipt.

05 · Signature element

The rate picker

One piece carries the whole direction: a plain, honest sliding scale you can read at a glance. Three rates, one season. The base rate is selected to start, because it is the true cost and the safest pick if you are not sure. Try it — it works with your keyboard and needs no login.

01 Same season, any rate

Appendix · About this direction

About this direction

Open to All is one of three directions built around the real assets from the current DiscNW site instead of tournament action photos. The job here is the real fee model. DiscNW uses a sliding scale, and this direction rebuilds it as clean, plain design rather than a dense graphic.

The rate picker is the whole idea in one piece. Three rates, one season, the base rate chosen to start. It is offered plainly, with the low rate stated as clearly as the high one. There is no questionnaire and no proof of income, because the goal is to remove a barrier, not add one.

The copy follows one rule: say the plain, true thing in short sentences. No hype, no big claims. A family reading this should feel like someone is talking straight with them about money, which is exactly the moment that decides whether a kid gets to play.

Choose this direction if…

You want the site to answer the cost question before a family has to ask it. This direction leads with the real sliding scale, states the $33 floor and financial aid plainly, and makes the low rate feel like a normal choice. It is the front door for a nonprofit whose whole vision is a community without barriers.

Think twice if…

You want the homepage to open with the sport at full volume. This direction leads with money and warmth, not action. The game is here, in the doors and the stories, but the hero is about access. If you need the first screen to feel like a highlight, another direction fits better.

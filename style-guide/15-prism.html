About this direction

15 — Prism

White light holds every color — so does this community. The site is a prism: a beam of white enters, splits into the full flowing spectrum, and you scroll down through the light. It is the kit's most immersive page, a spectral light show — but a disciplined one, grounded in a real idea. DiscNW's vision is an Ultimate community without barriers, so the rainbow reads as belonging: every color is a different kind of player, and all of them belong to the same beam.

The mechanism
A full-page flowing-spectrum aurora on near-black: a slow blurred spectrum wheel emanating from a fixed white light source, three drifting aurora blobs, and an optional canvas light-field on top. Everything moves on transform, opacity and filter only, eased over 30–46 seconds; scroll nudges the hue a few degrees and never touches the scrollbar. With reduced motion or no JavaScript it settles to one static spectral field and every word stays legible.
Signature
The prism split — a white beam fanning into the full spectrum — from the hero down through every section transition, so the story keeps emerging out of the light. The white formline mark is held in clearspace as the light source the spectrum flows from, landing large at the close above Every color. One community.
Palette
Void #0A0A12 Light source #FFFFFF full spectrum
Type
Outfit — a light, airy geometric grotesk carrying the display at enormous scale, thin as a beam of light · Hanken Grotesk — a clean, highly legible body on the dark glass · Spline Sans Mono — the wavelength readouts and labels.
Motion
Two house curves for choreography, one symmetric curve for the ambient drift; the browser default never appears. Text never rides the moving field — it sits on dark glass, or steps out into daylight for this appendix. A settle sweep lands everything on-screen by ≤2.3 seconds.
Accessibility
Body and UI hold AA on near-solid panels (white on panel ≈ 18:1); spectrum hues appear as text only in light tints that clear AA, never as small text and never over the churn. One h1, semantic landmarks, described alt text on every photo, and visible focus rings throughout.
Choose this if
You want the boldest, most emotional front door in the kit — one that makes without barriers something a visitor can see in five seconds: white light holding every color, and a community that does the same.
Think twice if
You need a dense utility homepage — standings, schedules, dozens of links above the fold — or you can't guarantee editors will keep text on the panels. A spectral light show wants very few things on stage and real discipline to stay legible.

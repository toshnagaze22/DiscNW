Flight Path

The current brand, evolved. Keep the navy members trust; modernize everything around it with sky, space, and one graphic idea — the arc of a disc in flight.

  • Familiar
  • Airborne
  • Systematic
  • Calm
  • Product-grade

02 · The idea

Don't replace the brand. Give it lift.

Over 10,000 players a year already know DiscNW navy — it's on their schedules, their registration receipts, their league emails. This direction keeps that navy as the anchor and rebuilds everything around it: a tier of sky blues above it, generous white space, and interface polish at the level of a product people use every week, because that's what this site is.

The one new idea is the flight path — the long hyzer arc of a thrown disc — used as the connective thread at every scale, from the hero animation down to a link underline. It's the direction that can ship page by page next to the existing registration tools, and the easiest for the board to say yes to.

Why this fits DiscNW

  • The navy stays — nothing to relearn for existing members, coaches, or captains.
  • Sky blues come straight from the sport: a white disc against a Seattle sky.
  • One arc, many scales — a system, not a decoration, so staff can apply it consistently.
  • Ships incrementally: new pages can sit beside current league-management pages without clashing.

05 · Signature element

The flight path

One drawn line — the long, smooth hyzer arc of a thrown disc — repeated at every scale: traced across the hero on page load, tucked under headings as an underline, stretched between sections as a divider, and bent into progress meters.

10 · Voice & tone

Plain verbs, warm sidelines.

The voice members already trust, tuned: active, specific, never precious. It sounds like the person at the fields who hands you a disc and points you to your team.

Hero headline

Your season starts here.

Registration confirmation

You're on the roster for Summer Mixed Hat League. First pull is Monday, June 1 at Magnuson Park — your schedule lives under your name, top right, same as always.

To a nervous first-time parent

Ultimate is non-contact and self-officiated, and nobody expects your kid to know a huck from a hat league on day one. Send cleats, water, and a lunch — we'll take care of the rest.

Choose this direction if…

You want the redesign members recognize on day one and the board approves in one meeting — the same trusted navy with product-grade polish, shippable page by page alongside the existing registration tools.

Think twice if…

You're hoping this redesign announces a bold new chapter — an evolved navy will read as a refresh, not a reinvention, and it stays in visual territory other sports leagues already occupy.

About this direction

24 · Home Squad

The idea

Ultimate is self-officiated. There are no referees, so the players, coaches, and volunteers are the institution. This direction celebrates that community as a set of collectible foil trading cards. Fronts hold a clean headshot, a name, a role, a program series, and a short Spirit-of-the-Game stat line. Backs hold a short story and a stat block. The pitch: collect the community, complete the set, and add yourself.

Signature

The Card of the Week. It tilts in 3D toward your pointer with a moving specular highlight and a cool holo sheen, and it flips on click to reveal the story on the back. The foil and shine live only on the card chrome: every photo sits in an opaque frame painted above the shimmer, so nothing ever crosses a face. With no JavaScript or reduced motion it sits flat and still flips on tap.

Palette · card-shop navy & cool foil

Deep navy #0E1630 Card-stock #EDEFF2 Cool holo foil silver→blue→mint Amber (rookie/star) #E0A32E Teal (stats) #22B8A6

Type

Archivo at heavy, expanded weights carries the names and headers with sports-card punch. Hanken Grotesk handles body text. JetBrains Mono sets every stat line and card number, like #009 / 250, so the numbers feel printed.

Motion

Two house curves for choreography, one symmetric curve for the foil shimmer. The card tilts and flips; cards fade up in a tight stagger; the set filters with pure CSS. Reduced motion removes the tilt and shimmer, and the settle lands everything on screen by about two seconds.

Accessibility

Body and UI clear WCAG AA on both grounds (text on navy ≈ 15:1; ink on card-stock ≈ 15:1). The flip is a focusable checkbox control; the set filters are radio buttons that work by keyboard; every headshot has descriptive alt text and is held at or below its native size. One h1, semantic landmarks, visible amber focus rings.

Choose this if

You want the front door to feel like a thing people collect: a warm, gamified way to say the community is the institution, and an easy on-ramp to "add your card."

Placeholder data

The roles, program series, card numbers, home fields, and the playful stat lines are placeholders to show the format. Swap in actual ones, and get each person's OK, before anything ships.

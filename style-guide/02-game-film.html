01 The idea

Treat them like athletes

Most rec-sports sites talk down to their players. This direction assumes the opposite: the people on DiscNW fields study matchups, argue about force angles, and drive to Ocean Shores for a beach tournament — so the site treats them like athletes. Play diagrams, film-room contrast, and honest numbers make the craft of ultimate visible, and Spirit of the Game runs through everything as the sport's code of honor — the thing that makes the competition trustworthy, not the thing that waters it down.

04 Signature element

The playbook diagram

X's and O's drawn in code, not stock art: a chalk field grid, dashed cuts, and one orange flight line. It scales from a full hero play down to a bullet-point icon — a graphic language no other sport in Seattle could claim.

Play 07 — vert stack, deep strike. Chalk O's cut, turf-green X's chase, cone-orange flies. Field 70 × 40 yd · end zones 25 yd

The cutterIcon-size play: one O, one dashed cut. Marks player-facing links and lists.

The clipboardCoaching Collective mark: a play mid-draw. Flags drills, plans, curricula.

The sidelineFamily territory: a cone and two spectators. Marks parent guides and logistics.

07 Storytelling component

The film room

Stories told the way this audience already talks: a clip, a timecode breakdown, and the quote that makes it matter. The same template serves a first layout, a coach's favorite drill, or a spirit circle that fixed a game. Below it, the org's numbers get the same narrative treatment.

Photo treatment — real frame
YCC tryout film: a forehand out against a tight mark, disc mid-flight. Navy-and-orange duotone, high contrast — grayscale pushed to night navy, cone orange screened in from one corner, chalk scan-lines kept. Every photo on the site gets the same film pass.

09 Voice & tone

Coach's voice, athlete's ear

Direct, specific, a little dry. Numbers whenever they earn their place. Warmth shows up as respect — the site assumes you can handle real information.

Hero headline

Train all winter. Prove it at Potlatch.

Season-arc framing: every league is a build toward something.

Registration confirmation

Roster locked. You're in for Summer Mixed Hat League — first pull is June 2. Your schedule posts under your name, top right, about two weeks out.

Confirmation reads like a team announcement, not a receipt.

To a nervous first-time parent

Ultimate is non-contact, and the kids referee themselves — it teaches honest competition faster than any whistle could. Send cleats, water, and a rain layer. We'll teach the rest, including you.

Reassurance through facts, closed with one line of warmth.

10 The honest read

Is Game Film your direction?

Choose this direction if…

Your first job is convincing competitive players, captains, and ambitious teenagers that DiscNW takes the sport as seriously as they do — and you want parents to read that seriousness as legitimacy.

Think twice if…

The nervous eight-year-old and the brand-new parent are your front door — film-room intensity can read as a tryout rather than a welcome, and this direction needs discipline to stay friendly at the edges.

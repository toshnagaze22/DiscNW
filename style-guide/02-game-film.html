01The idea

Treat them like athletes

Most rec-sports sites talk down to their players. This direction assumes the opposite: the people on DiscNW fields study matchups, argue about force angles, and drive to Ocean Shores for a beach tournament — so the site treats them like athletes. One play diagram drawn in chalk, film presented straight, numbers set like a box score. Spirit of the Game runs through everything as the sport's code of honor — the thing that makes the competition trustworthy, not the thing that waters it down.

09Voice & tone

Coach's voice, athlete's ear

Direct, specific, a little dry. Numbers whenever they earn their place. Warmth shows up as respect — the site assumes you can handle real information.

Hero headline

Train all winter. Prove it at Potlatch.

Season-arc framing: every league is a build toward something.

Registration confirmation

Roster locked. You're in for Summer Mixed Hat League — first pull is June 2. Your schedule posts under your name, top right, about two weeks out.

Confirmation reads like a team announcement, not a receipt.

To a nervous first-time parent

Ultimate is non-contact, and the kids referee themselves — it teaches honest competition faster than any whistle could. Send cleats, water, and a rain layer. We'll teach the rest, including you.

Reassurance through facts, closed with one line of warmth.

10The honest read

Is Game Film your direction?

Choose this direction if…

Your first job is convincing competitive players, captains, and ambitious teenagers that DiscNW takes the sport as seriously as they do — and you want parents to read that seriousness as legitimacy.

Think twice if…

The nervous eight-year-old and the brand-new parent are your front door — film-room intensity can read as a tryout rather than a welcome, and this direction needs discipline to stay friendly at the edges.

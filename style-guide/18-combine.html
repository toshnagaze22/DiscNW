01The idea

Put the numbers on the jumbotron

Most nonprofits bury their scale in a footer. This one earns a HUD.

DiscNW isn't small. Ten thousand-plus players a year. Thirty years of leagues since the first Potlatch in 1995. And Spring Reign — the largest mixed youth ultimate tournament on the planet. Those facts are the story, so this direction refuses to whisper them.

Combine borrows the language people already read adrenaline in: F1 telemetry, the NFL Combine readout, a WHOOP dashboard, the ESPN stat bug. Numbers count up and slam. Gauges wind to their marks. A leaderboard reads like it just refreshed. You don't tell a parent this community is huge and serious — you let the data hit them.

It stays lawful the whole way: photos and film run clean on solid mats, the crest keeps its clearspace, and with no JavaScript every total already shows its final figure. The hype is real, not fragile.

02Palette

Dark telemetry, one volt signal

A near-black instrument panel, a stack of charcoals for depth, and one electric data accent — volt green — carrying every live value, gauge, and call to action. Cyan, amber, and redline appear only inside the gauges as a strict WHOOP-style status grammar. Photos bring all remaining color themselves, untouched.

THE accent. Live values, filled gauges, the Register button, focus rings. Nothing decorative.

Secondary telemetry — growth bars and one gauge. Never a CTA.

Redline

The live bug and critical states only. Rare on purpose.

#9BA8BA

Body copy and every monospace label, timecode, and unit.

03Typography

Grotesk that slams, mono that reads

09Voice & tone

Loud on the numbers, warm on the people

Combine shouts the stats and then gets out of the way. When a real person is on the other end — a nervous parent, a new registrant — the volume drops and the warmth carries.

Hero headline

Train all winter. Clock in at Potlatch.

Season-arc framing — every league builds toward a marquee weekend.

Registration confirmation

Roster locked. You're in for Summer Mixed Hat League — first pull is June 2 at Magnuson. Your schedule posts under your name about two weeks out.

Reads like a team announcement, not a receipt.

To a nervous first-time parent

Ultimate is non-contact, and the kids referee themselves — it teaches honest competition faster than any whistle could. Send cleats, water, and a rain layer. We'll teach the rest, including you.

Facts first, closed with one line of warmth.

10The honest read

Is Combine your direction?

Choose this if…

You want the redesign to lead with scale — to make a parent, a sponsor, or a first-time visitor feel the size and seriousness of DiscNW in the first three seconds. When the numbers are your best argument, put them on the jumbotron and let them slam.

Think twice if…

Your front door needs to feel quiet, soft, and human before anything else. A dark neon HUD is a stadium entrance — thrilling, but loud. For an audience that wants a calm hand on the shoulder first, a gentler direction opens warmer.

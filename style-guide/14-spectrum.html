02 — The idea

Everyone belongs here.

DiscNW’s vision is four words: an Ultimate community without barriers. Wide Open takes them to heart. If everyone belongs, the brand has to feel open to all of them — the seven-year-old at her first camp, the masters hat-leaguer, the coach, the parent learning the rules from the sideline. Every audience has a place here, and none of them sits at the edge. It’s built to feel like a well-run festival, not a flag: the colour lives in light and line, the type stays precise, and the photography is left completely alone. Joy, done with taste.

03 — Palette

A tuned palette, anchored in ink.

Seven jewel tones tuned to sit together — no default primaries, nothing garish. The vivid palette lives in light, ribbons, chips, and focus rings; it never runs under body copy. When a colour has to be read as text, it drops to an AA-safe ink. Photographs keep their own colour, always.

Every “as text” ink above is checked against paper at 4.5:1 or better (indigo and violet clear 7:1). The bright originals never carry small type — that discipline is what keeps a seven-colour system from turning garish.

04 — Typography

Joyful precision.

Hanken Grotesk carries the joy — a warm grotesk that reads rounded at a glance and sharp up close. Figtree does the quiet work: schedules, waivers, fee tables. A friendly-precise pairing that never tips into novelty.

05 — Signature element

The signature ribbon.

One device ties the whole system together: a single, smooth ribbon that carries the whole palette. It underlines the wordmark, divides the sections, and above all becomes the belonging bar. Its hue drifts on a long, slow loop, then settles to a single static gradient if you prefer less motion.

Under the wordmark

The lockup is never recoloured or filtered — the colour lives on the keyline beneath it, so the wordmark stays official and the belonging reads underneath.

As a section divider

Three weights of the same idea. The ribbon carries the light between bands so the pages breathe colour without ever tinting a word or a photograph.

Motion rule: the ribbon flows in a slow 26-second loop, never a flash. The belonging bar grows once from the baseline when it scrolls in, then holds. With motion turned down, all of it freezes to the static palette you see here.

10 — Voice & tone

How Wide Open sounds.

Warm, direct, and openly welcoming — it talks like a captain saving you a spot on the sideline, not like league-management software. Joyful, never saccharine.

Hero headline

Everyone belongs on this field.

Registration confirmation

You’re in — Summer Mixed Hat League, first pull June 1. We’ll email your team after draft night. No experience required; a sideline full of new names is.

To a nervous first-time parent

You don’t need to know what a huck is. Bring a camp chair and a water bottle — the coach handles the rest, and the sideline always has room for one more.

Choose this direction if…

You want the brand to say “everyone” out loud — a joyful, premium, inclusive system that makes “without barriers” something a visitor feels in the first second, without ever tipping into novelty.

Think twice if…

You want one ownable brand colour and a quiet, corporate restraint. Wide Open leans all the way into colour and joy — it asks every future editor to protect the taste that keeps it a festival identity, not a flag.

Evergreen

Pacific Northwest place-identity. Ultimate as this region plays it — the brand is rooted in where the game happens: wet grass, fog, firs, and long June light, year-round.

Rooted·Weatherproof·Local·Year-round·Welcoming

02 — The idea

Belonging to a place

Most league sites could be anywhere. DiscNW couldn’t: this game lives at Magnuson Park, in fog at a 9 a.m. jamboree, in June light that holds until the last point. Evergreen makes that the brand — fir green and fog grey doing the everyday work, one vivid wet-grass green marking every action, and a drawn treeline putting every page somewhere real. The photographs stay untouched, because the place is already in them. The bet is simple: people don’t just sign up for a league, they join a place.

05 — Signature element

The horizon line

One device, drawn once, used everywhere: four grove layers and two banks of fog, built from three fir silhouettes. It opens the page, grounds the hero, divides the light sections from the dark, and closes the footer. It is drawn — never printed over a photograph.

Drawn, not photographed. Far ridge, fog, mid grove, fog, near grove, front grove — one shared set of SVG symbols. Static by design · the weather does the moving

07 — Audience pathways

Three front doors

Players, coaches, parents — each gets their own door, in their own language, three seconds after the page loads. Photography above, words below; the two never fight.

Players

Your next team is one signup away.

Hat league, team league, pickup, or a beach weekend at Sea Plastic — year-round, rec to competitive. Tell us the season and how you like to play; we’ll show you where you fit.

Coaches & volunteers

You don’t have to be elite. You have to show up.

The Coaching Collective backs you with trainings, practice plans, and a bench of coaches to lean on — from your first background check to the season’s last spirit circle.

Parents & families

New to ultimate? Most of our parents were.

Learn the game in five minutes, get the gear list, and hear the honest answer to “where are the referees?” — there aren’t any, and it’s the best thing about this sport.

10 — Voice & tone

How Evergreen sounds

Plainspoken, weatherproof, a little wry. It talks like a longtime captain welcoming you to the sideline — never like league-management software.

Hero headline

Ultimate grows here.

Registration confirmation

You’re in — Summer Wonder League, starting June 1. We’ll email your team after draft night. Bring cleats: the grass will be wet, and that’s the point.

To a nervous first-time parent

You don’t need to know what a huck is yet. Bring a rain jacket and a camp chair; your kid’s coach handles the rest, and the sideline will save you a spot.

Choose this direction if…

You want a brand that could only belong to Puget Sound ultimate — instantly familiar to anyone who’s played a March game in the rain, and warm enough to hand a nervous parent.

Think twice if…

You want the site to lead with scores, speed, and sports-tech energy — Evergreen leads with place and belonging, and it will always feel more forest than stadium.

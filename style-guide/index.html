DiscNW — Design directions

Nineteen directions for the discnw.org redesign, each built around storytelling for players, coaches, and parents. They're sorted into five families — Place & weather, Sport & broadcast, People & community, System & utility, and Gameday, the loudest bring-the-energy tier — each opening with the question it answers about how discnw.org should feel; read a family top to bottom, where a small badge marks every direction as either a grounded, shippable design system or an experimental, single-mechanism homepage. Every direction is built on real DiscNW photography and film, with the formline mark integrated throughout and motion choreography informed by Awwwards Site-of-the-Year winners. Each card previews that direction's redesigned homepage; open it to explore the full page live. Open each one and react honestly — first impressions count. Nothing here is all-or-nothing: directions can be mixed, and a grounded system can adopt an experimental page's mechanism.

Place & weather

Does the site feel like HERE? Directions rooted in Puget Sound — its rain, light, forests, and seasons — so the brand could only belong to Seattle ultimate.

DIRECTION 01

Evergreen

Grounded

Rooted in the place we play

Ultimate as the Pacific Northwest plays it — the brand is rooted in the wet grass, fog, firs, and long June light where the game actually happens.

Type: Archivo (expanded, 700–900) + Source Sans 3

Signature: Real weather as the structure — fog and old-growth photography as full-bleed atmosphere bands with quiet fir-plate captions, the formline mark crest-quiet on deep fir, and weather-paced atmosphere choreography (mask-rise masthead, clip-path band wipes, magnetic CTAs) on one house easing language.

Feels like: weather moving through — bands wipe in like lifting fog, type rises out of the mist, and buttons lean gently toward your hand.

Choose if: you want a brand that could only belong to Puget Sound ultimate — instantly familiar to anyone who's played a March game in the rain, and warm enough to hand a nervous parent.

Think twice if: you want the site to lead with scores, speed, and sports-tech energy — Evergreen leads with place and belonging, and it will always feel more forest than stadium.

Game to 15

Experimental

The whole site is a game to 15

The site is a game of Ultimate played to 15: a fixed scoreboard scores your reading as fifteen colossal numbered truths carry you from the first pull to a full-screen game point — the whole personality lives in the typesetting.

Palette
  • Line Chalk #FAFAF7

  • Universe Orange #FF4A00

Type: Space Grotesk (colossal tabular numerals and the scoreboard) + Newsreader (the editorial columns, sharp and small)

Signature: The crested score strip — "DISCNW 0 — 0 YOU" now carrying an equalizer, a pulsing LIVE bug and a momentum meter — tallies your reading. Each colossal numeral lands on an overshoot pop and detonates an abstract ring + multi-color spark burst in its own accent (indigo, tidal, orange), a halftime video highlight breaks in mid-run, the final points run hot, and at 15 the orange still floods down out of the scoreboard itself.

The mechanism: a fixed scoreboard scores a point each time a numbered section crosses mid-viewport — scroll back and the point un-scores — with a static strip for reduced motion and no-JS.

Feels like: a live game building to universe point — scoreboard kinetics with the crowd turned up. Numbers slam and spark as they land, each point flashes its own color, the momentum meter fills and the LIVE strip surges through the hot final stretch, until point 15 erupts into a full-screen orange flood.

Choose if: you want the page a jury retells in one sentence — "the site is a game to 15" — pure typography turned into a live broadcast: the cheapest hype-tier direction to build flawlessly, with the sport's own structure doing the branding.

Think twice if: you need a working homepage, not a manifesto — schedules, standings and registration paths still require one of the grounded directions underneath this louder voice.

Type: Anton (single heavy condensed weight — the drop poster, section titles, every countdown digit) + Archivo (400–900, body, UI, tabular figures)

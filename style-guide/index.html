DiscNW — Design directions

Thirteen directions for the discnw.org redesign, each built around storytelling for players, coaches, and parents. They're sorted into four families — Place & weather, Sport & broadcast, People & community, and System & utility — each opening with the question it answers about how discnw.org should feel; read a family top to bottom, where a small badge marks every direction as either a grounded, shippable design system or an experimental, single-mechanism homepage. Every direction is built on real DiscNW photography and film, with the formline mark integrated throughout and motion choreography informed by Awwwards Site-of-the-Year winners. Each card previews that direction's redesigned homepage; open it to explore the full page live. Open each one and react honestly — first impressions count. Nothing here is all-or-nothing: directions can be mixed, and a grounded system can adopt an experimental page's mechanism.

Place & weather

Does the site feel like HERE? Directions rooted in Puget Sound — its rain, light, forests, and seasons — so the brand could only belong to Seattle ultimate.

DIRECTION 01

Evergreen

Grounded

Rooted in the place we play

Ultimate as the Pacific Northwest plays it — the brand is rooted in the wet grass, fog, firs, and long June light where the game actually happens.

Palette
  • Old Fir #14291F
  • Hemlock #24513B
  • Wet Grass #3DAE54
  • Fog Bank #94A9B4
  • Morning Fog #F1F3EE
  • Rain Slate #44554D

Type: Archivo (expanded, 700–900) + Source Sans 3

Signature: Real weather as the structure — fog and old-growth photography as full-bleed atmosphere bands with quiet fir-plate captions, the formline mark crest-quiet on deep fir, and weather-paced atmosphere choreography (mask-rise masthead, clip-path band wipes, magnetic CTAs) on one house easing language.

Feels like: weather moving through — bands wipe in like lifting fog, type rises out of the mist, and buttons lean gently toward your hand.

Choose if: you want a brand that could only belong to Puget Sound ultimate — instantly familiar to anyone who's played a March game in the rain, and warm enough to hand a nervous parent.

Think twice if: you want the site to lead with scores, speed, and sports-tech energy — Evergreen leads with place and belonging, and it will always feel more forest than stadium.

DIRECTION 07

The Understory

Experimental

The place is the brand, photographed in the field

A photographic field guide to the Pacific Northwest season, kept in the WPA park-poster voice: the place is the brand, and the season is a trail map through it.

Palette — poster inks
  • Paper Cream #F5EEDA
  • Deep Fir #1F3A2E
  • Glacier #7E9AA8
  • Sunset Coral #AE4527
  • Meadow #5B8A4A

Type: Big Shoulders (WPA condensed caps) + Source Serif 4 (the field-guide voice)

Signature: A photographic field guide to the season — real fog-and-old-growth imagery carries the place while one dashed coral trail draws itself down the year, waypoint photographs develop like specimen prints, and the formline mark stands as the trailhead medallion.

The mechanism: one scroll-linked trail — the dashed coral route draws itself down the year while the fog-forest hero drifts in real-photo parallax, transform-only and clamped — settling to a fully drawn route without JS or with reduced motion.

Feels like: a walk down the trail — the route draws underfoot, waypoints pin-drop into place, and specimen photographs develop like prints in the tray.

Choose if: you want an identity no other sports organization in America could copy — place-first, craft-heavy, warm to parents and volunteers, and endlessly extensible into posters, patches, stamps and sideline merch.

Think twice if: you need score-forward, sports-tech energy or fast template production — here the field-guide craft is the brand, and every major new page asks for photography worth pinning.

Rain or Shine

Grounded

Seattle weather turned from apology into credential

Palette
  • Front Ink #161D24
  • Overcast Paper #EDF0F1
  • Radar Green #3BD97F
  • Wet-Season Green #0C6E3E
  • Sunbreak #F5AE3D
  • Barometer Slate #46545F

Type: Space Grotesk + IBM Plex Mono

Signature: A meteorological field bulletin that treats Seattle weather as a credential rather than an apology — anchored by a live conditions board where you read straight down the status column and keep finding the same word: ON.

Feels like: a departures board ticking through field status on the hour, then going dead still.

Choose if: You want a brand no other league could wear — Seattle weather turned from an excuse into a credential, a live conditions board that answers the only question players ask at dawn, and data warm enough to reassure a parent. It could only belong to ultimate in the Puget Sound rain.

Think twice if: Your front door needs sun-and-smiles reassurance first. A monospace bulletin leads with facts and instruments, and its charm is dry — it can read cool to a brand-new family for a beat before the devotion in the data lands.

DIRECTION 13

Last Light

Experimental

One scroll through a whole year of Seattle light

Palette
  • Zenith Ink #14112A
  • Nine-PM Gold #E1955A
  • October Amber #D08238
  • Blue Hour #3C5480
  • Returning Peach #F0B87E

Type: Fraunces + Spline Sans Mono

Signature: The page is a year-long light clock — five real Seattle skies crossfaded by scroll, every DiscNW program placed at the hour of light it is actually played in, and a year that deliberately closes brightening at Spring Reign rather than darkening.

Feels like: the sky easing through a whole year of light as you scroll — dusk to noon to blue hour and back to spring.

Choose if: The calendar is your best argument — you want visitors to feel why a Seattle season is worth registering for before anyone tells them, and one scroll to explain the entire year, ending on the spring CTA.

Think twice if: You need a dense utility homepage — standings, schedules, dozens of links above the fold. A light clock wants very few things on stage at once, and its gradients ask for restraint from every future content editor.

Sport & broadcast

Does it feel like the GAME? Directions that speak to the athlete — the film room, the flight of a single point, the scoreboard — giving a rec-league game the seriousness players feel.

DIRECTION 02

Game Film

Grounded

The sport taken seriously, chalk and floodlights

DiscNW players train, scheme, and compete — so the site speaks to the athlete with playbook diagrams, film-room contrast, and stat culture, with Spirit of the Game as its code of honor rather than a softener.

Palette
  • Night Game #0B1626
  • Field Navy #11243D
  • Chalk #F2F6F8
  • Cone Orange #FF6B1C
  • Turf Line #6F9E83
  • Press Box Slate #8FA3BA

Type: Barlow Condensed + Inter

Signature: Kinetic broadcast — the page opens like a title sequence, headlines slide from masks, the box score rolls to its totals, and hovering real game film scrubs it like a coach at the tape — every frame signed by the formline crest.

Feels like: floodlit precision — one easing language, 90ms staggers, digits rolling like an odometer before landing true, and film that answers your pointer like a jog wheel.

Choose if: your first job is convincing competitive players, captains, and ambitious teenagers that DiscNW takes the sport as seriously as they do — and you want parents to read that seriousness as legitimacy.

Think twice if: the nervous eight-year-old and the brand-new parent are your front door — film-room intensity can read as a tryout rather than a welcome, and this direction needs discipline to stay friendly at the edges.

DIRECTION 06

One Point

Experimental

A single point of ultimate, narrated pull to catch

The page is a single point of ultimate, narrated pull to catch: one ember flight line draws itself down the whole story as you scroll, while the evening deepens from dusk to field-light navy around it.

Palette
  • First Dark #070D1E
  • Field Navy #16203F
  • Duskline #59303F
  • Chalk #F2EEE3
  • Ember #FF6A2B

Type: Big Shoulders Display + Schibsted Grotesk, with Spline Sans Mono for the broadcast chyrons

Signature: One point, pull to catch — an ember flight line drags with real momentum past mask-revealed headlines while a crest-badged broadcast bug calls the stations, until the cursor becomes the disc and the corner flips CAUGHT — GAME ON.

The mechanism: one velocity-lagged motion system — the flight line and its disc lerp toward your scroll instead of tracking it 1:1, while the corner chyron rolls at every station; without JS, in print, or with reduced motion, the route renders fully flown.

Feels like: thrown, not tracked — the line lags your scroll and catches up, the chyron cuts to the next play, and the dusk glow eases out as you descend.

Choose if: you want the presentation people retell the next morning — a front door that argues, in one continuous gesture, that a rec league point deserves broadcast treatment, and that makes registering feel like catching the goal.

Think twice if: you need workhorse information architecture first — this is a narrative cover, not a schedule tool, and standings, rosters, and rainout utilities still need one of the grounded systems behind it.

Open 06 — One Point
DIRECTION 08

Game to 15

Experimental

The whole site is a game to 15

The site is a game of Ultimate played to 15: a fixed scoreboard scores your reading as fifteen colossal numbered truths carry you from the first pull to a full-screen game point — the whole personality lives in the typesetting.

Palette
  • Line Chalk #FAFAF7
  • Stat Ink #101010
  • Universe Orange #FF4A00

Type: Space Grotesk (colossal tabular numerals and the scoreboard) + Newsreader (the editorial columns, sharp and small)

Signature: The crested score strip — "DISCNW 0 — 0 YOU" scoring your reading while colossal numerals land under rising masks, until at point 15 the orange floods down out of the strip itself.

The mechanism: a fixed scoreboard scores a point each time a numbered section crosses mid-viewport — scroll back and the point un-scores — with a static strip for reduced motion and no-JS.

Feels like: scoreboard kinetics — each point ticks with a digit pulse, numerals land with a slight overshoot, and the point-15 flood rewinds truthfully if you scroll back.

Choose if: you want the page a jury retells in one sentence — "the site is a game to 15" — pure typography, cheapest to build flawlessly, and the sport's own structure doing the branding.

Think twice if: you need a working homepage, not a manifesto — schedules, standings, and registration paths still require one of the grounded directions underneath this voice.

People & community

Does it feel like US? Directions that lead with the people — real voices, a Saturday sideline, ten thousand first names — so every program reads as what it gives a person.

DIRECTION 03

Sideline Day

Grounded

Saturday at the fields, zero intimidation

The truest picture of DiscNW is a Saturday at the fields — camp chairs, orange slices, a first layout — so the site should feel like being welcomed onto that sideline, built first for parents and brand-new players.

Palette
  • Field Green #1F7A33
  • Clear Sky #1D6FB5
  • Marigold #FFB61E
  • Evergreen Ink #1B3022
  • Daylight White #FFFFFF
  • Morning Sky #EAF4FC

Type: Baloo 2 + Atkinson Hyperlegible

Signature: The sunny sticker-sheet welcome — die-cut water-bottle badges curated like a good sticker sheet, where the DiscNW crest signs the page at an easy 2° and every badge lands with a spring.

Feels like: springy and tactile — stickers pop in with peel-and-press physics, the hero photo lifts its curtain, and marigold highlighter sweeps under every overline.

Choose if: growing the community is the job — you want the site that recruits new families and first-time players hardest, and makes "no barriers" something a visitor can feel in the first five seconds.

Think twice if: your competitive club players and tournament veterans need to see themselves first — this direction leads with newcomers, and elite intensity deliberately rides in the back seat.

Open 03 — Sideline Day
DIRECTION 04

Spirit & Story

Grounded

Every program is a person's story

The site reads like a beautifully edited community magazine — real voices at headline scale, documentary photography, Spirit of the Game as the editorial voice — so every page answers "what will this give my kid?" with a person, not a pitch.

Palette
  • Pull-Sky Paper #FAFAF7
  • Puget Ink #10192B
  • Spirit Green #0E5F41
  • Sideline Grey #5E635A
  • Grass Tint #E9F0E9

Type: Newsreader + Public Sans

Signature: The community magazine that reads like print and moves like one being made — the oversized attributed pull quote ("— Priya S., Spring Reign parent") inks in line-by-line, photographs develop on arrival, rules draw themselves, and the formline mark is set like a press seal.

Feels like: a press run — ink dries line-by-line, prints come off the roller, and the colophon seal lands last.

Choose if: you want the site itself to prove the storytelling goal — every program introduced by a person who lived it — and you can commit to gathering a handful of real photos and quotes each season to keep the magazine honest.

Think twice if: registration throughput is the whole job this year: an editorial site needs a steady trickle of fresh stories, and a magazine with stale features reads worse than a plain catalog.

DIRECTION 11

First Names

Experimental

The community as a living roster of ten thousand names

Palette
  • Warm Paper #FAF4E8
  • Roster Ink #23201B
  • Camp Green #17703D
  • League Blue #2C55C4
  • Hat-League Brick #B23A1C

Type: Bricolage Grotesque + Instrument Sans

Signature: The community rendered as a living roster of ten thousand first names — a composed census as the hero, five names followed through real programs until the roster becomes a set of stories, every screen arguing toward one CTA: add your name.

Feels like: a crowd assembling — names rising in tight staggers, a census filling in, never confetti.

Choose if: You want the mission self-evident — people first, programs shown as what they do for a person — and a hero no other sports organization can copy without also having ten thousand names behind it.

Think twice if: You need program-first wayfinding on day one, or invented sample names would sit uneasily beside your real member data. This direction is strongest when the wall eventually goes real — opt-in first names from actual registration.

System & utility

Does it WORK harder? Directions that make the site itself do more — a trusted navy given lift, a transit map of every program, a numbered handbook — so visitors find the answer fast.

DIRECTION 05

Flight Path

Grounded

The brand you trust, given lift

Keep the navy 10,000 members already trust and give it lift — sky gradients, generous space, product-grade polish, and one disc-flight arc that ties every page together.

Palette
  • Anchor Navy #1c3b65
  • Night Flight #11243d
  • Cascade Blue #27528d
  • Midday Sky #5b93d1
  • Morning Sky #e7f1fb
  • Sunrise Gold #ffb43a

Type: Sora + Inter

Signature: The navy you trust with product-grade lift — the homepage assembles in one buttery 60ms-stagger pass on two house curves, one gold hyzer arc lands last, and the formline mark closes at crest scale.

Feels like: buttery product polish — cards tilt a few degrees under the pointer, gold buttons are gently magnetic, and the arc always draws itself last.

Choose if: you want the redesign members recognize on day one and the board approves in one meeting — the same trusted navy with product-grade polish, one disciplined signature, shippable page by page alongside the existing registration tools.

Think twice if: you're hoping this redesign announces a bold new chapter — an evolved navy will read as a refresh, not a reinvention, and it stays in visual territory other sports leagues already occupy.

Open 05 — Flight Path
DIRECTION 10

The Route Map

Grounded

The whole system drawn as one transit map

Palette
  • Signal Ink #14181D
  • Service White #F6F4EF
  • Youth Line #0C7A3F
  • School Line #C2410C
  • Adult Line #235BA8
  • Spirit Line #7C2D8E

Type: Overpass + Overpass Mono + Inter

Signature: Transit-system wayfinding that turns DiscNW's dozens of programs into one legible map — four color-coded lines, real programs as stations, the big tournaments as interchanges — so the site stops describing the system and starts being it.

Feels like: a transit map drawing itself — lines pulling out of the origin station, stops popping in travel order, YOU ARE HERE pulsing once.

Choose if: The loudest complaint about the current site is getting lost. The Route Map makes navigation itself the brand: every program gets an address on a line, every audience gets a door, and the system grows by adding stations — not redesigning pages.

Think twice if: You want the first feeling to be atmosphere and emotion. Signage is proudly utilitarian — if the destination photography ever thins out, this direction can read schedule-first, community-second.

Open 10 — The Route Map
DIRECTION 12

The Handbook

Grounded

The sport runs on a shared text — so does the site

Palette
  • Vellum #FAF6EC
  • Ink #221E18
  • Regulation Oxblood #77202C
  • Highlighter #FFE45C
  • Pencil #665E51
  • Buckram #EDE4D2

Type: Literata + IBM Plex Mono

Signature: A sport that runs on a shared text deserves a site that reads like one — discnw.org rebuilt as the community's beautifully typeset reference shelf, where numbered clauses, marginalia and cross-references are the information architecture, not decoration.

Feels like: a well-set manual laying its pages — clause numbers inking in left to right, and nothing else moving.

Choose if: Your visitors arrive with a question and a deadline — the parent at 9 p.m., the new coach mid-practice — and you want calm, numbered authority that makes every answer feel one lookup away, in the one sport that literally runs on a shared handbook.

Think twice if: You need the homepage to sell adrenaline — The Handbook is deliberately quiet, and its reference-desk welcome can read like a syllabus next to a poster to the highlight-reel teenager, or to a sponsor who wants spectacle above the fold.

DiscNW — Design directions

Five directions for the discnw.org redesign, each built around storytelling for players, coaches, and parents. Open each one and react honestly — first impressions count. Nothing here is all-or-nothing: directions can be mixed, like one direction's palette carrying another's storytelling components.

DIRECTION 01

Evergreen

Rooted in the place we play

Ultimate as the Pacific Northwest plays it — the brand is rooted in the wet grass, fog, firs, and long June light where the game actually happens.

Palette
  • Old Fir #14291F
  • Hemlock #24513B
  • Wet Grass #3DAE54
  • Fog Bank #94A9B4
  • Morning Fog #F1F3EE
  • Rain Slate #44554D

Type: Archivo (expanded, 700–900) + Source Sans 3

Signature: A layered fir–fog–field horizon in inline SVG that grounds the hero, divides sections, and closes the footer — the fog layer drifts gently on scroll.

Choose if: you want a brand that could only belong to Puget Sound ultimate — instantly familiar to anyone who's played a March game in the rain, and warm enough to hand a nervous parent.

Think twice if: you want the site to lead with scores, speed, and sports-tech energy — Evergreen leads with place and belonging, and it will always feel more forest than stadium.

DIRECTION 02

Game Film

The sport taken seriously, chalk and floodlights

DiscNW players train, scheme, and compete — so the site speaks to the athlete with playbook diagrams, film-room contrast, and stat culture, with Spirit of the Game as its code of honor rather than a softener.

Palette
  • Night Game #0B1626
  • Field Navy #11243D
  • Chalk #F2F6F8
  • Cone Orange #FF6B1C
  • Turf Line #6F9E83
  • Press Box Slate #8FA3BA

Type: Barlow Condensed + Inter

Signature: Play diagrams as a graphic language — X's and O's on a chalk field grid with dashed cutting arrows and one cone-orange huck line, from an animated hero play down to icon-size motifs.

Choose if: your first job is convincing competitive players, captains, and ambitious teenagers that DiscNW takes the sport as seriously as they do — and you want parents to read that seriousness as legitimacy.

Think twice if: the nervous eight-year-old and the brand-new parent are your front door — film-room intensity can read as a tryout rather than a welcome, and this direction needs discipline to stay friendly at the edges.

DIRECTION 03

Sideline Day

Saturday at the fields, zero intimidation

The truest picture of DiscNW is a Saturday at the fields — camp chairs, orange slices, a first layout — so the site should feel like being welcomed onto that sideline, built first for parents and brand-new players.

Palette
  • Field Green #1F7A33
  • Clear Sky #1D6FB5
  • Marigold #FFB61E
  • Evergreen Ink #1B3022
  • Daylight White #FFFFFF
  • Morning Sky #EAF4FC

Type: Baloo 2 + Atkinson Hyperlegible

Signature: A water-bottle sticker system — die-cut vinyl-style badges ("No experience needed", "Ages 7–18", a Spirit of the Game disc) slightly rotated, plus a hand-drawn disc-flight doodle as headline underline and divider.

Choose if: growing the community is the job — you want the site that recruits new families and first-time players hardest, and makes "no barriers" something a visitor can feel in the first five seconds.

Think twice if: your competitive club players and tournament veterans need to see themselves first — this direction leads with newcomers, and elite intensity deliberately rides in the back seat.

DIRECTION 04

Spirit & Story

Every program is a person's story

The site reads like a beautifully edited community magazine — real voices at headline scale, documentary photography, Spirit of the Game as the editorial voice — so every page answers "what will this give my kid?" with a person, not a pitch.

Palette
  • Pull-Sky Paper #FAFAF7
  • Puget Ink #10192B
  • Spirit Green #0E5F41
  • Sideline Grey #5E635A
  • Grass Tint #E9F0E9

Type: Newsreader + Public Sans

Signature: The oversized attributed pull quote — a real community voice at 60–90px italic with a fixed byline convention ("— Maya R., Fall Middle School League parent") — used everywhere a slogan would normally go.

Choose if: you want the site itself to prove the storytelling goal — every program introduced by a person who lived it — and you can commit to gathering a handful of real photos and quotes each season to keep the magazine honest.

Think twice if: registration throughput is the whole job this year: an editorial site needs a steady trickle of fresh stories, and a magazine with stale features reads worse than a plain catalog.

Open 04 — Spirit & Story
DIRECTION 05

Flight Path

The brand you trust, given lift

Keep the navy 10,000 members already trust and give it lift — sky gradients, generous space, product-grade polish, and one disc-flight arc that ties every page together.

Palette
  • Anchor Navy #1c3b65
  • Night Flight #11243d
  • Cascade Blue #27528d
  • Midday Sky #5b93d1
  • Morning Sky #e7f1fb
  • Sunrise Gold #ffb43a

Type: Sora + Inter

Signature: The flight path — the long hyzer arc of a thrown disc as one drawn line at every scale: traced by a gold dot across the hero, tucked under headings, stretched between sections, bent into capacity meters and a season timeline.

Choose if: you want the redesign members recognize on day one and the board approves in one meeting — the same trusted navy with product-grade polish, shippable page by page alongside the existing registration tools.

Think twice if: you're hoping this redesign announces a bold new chapter — an evolved navy will read as a refresh, not a reinvention, and it stays in visual territory other sports leagues already occupy.

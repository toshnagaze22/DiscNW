May · Skagit Valley

The mountains watched the whole time

Every spring the season moves north to the Skagit Valley for Spring Reign. One weekend, every age bracket, a disc in the air at all times, and foothills on the horizon while kids play the best games of their year.

It is the world's largest mixed youth ultimate tournament, and it still feels like a field trip. You come home tired, sunburned, and already counting to next May.

June · around Seattle

Nobody is born knowing how to throw

Summer is the first-timers' season. Summer Youth Ultimate Camps take players ages 7 to 18 at every level, and the Wonder League keeps the evenings low-key. The disc goes up, somebody drops it, everybody laughs, you go again.

Fees start at $33 on a sliding scale, no questions asked. All tiers get the identical week.

All season · every field

No referees. That is the point

Ultimate has zero referees. Players make their own calls, at every level, in every division. It is called Spirit of the Game, and it is really just a way of saying everyone belongs on the field.

Games here all end the same way, both teams through one tunnel of high-fives. Ask a player in November what stuck with them; usually it is this.

25 · Field Notes

A season of ultimate, kept the way you'd keep a trip: as a passport. Tournaments are places you travel to, and each trip adds a stamp to the book. It is warm, analog, and personal, a keepsake rather than a brochure. It leans on DiscNW's vision, an Ultimate community without barriers, by making the whole year feel like somewhere anyone can go.

The mechanism
Each destination is a rubber stamp, drawn in CSS and SVG with a roughened ink edge. As each one scrolls into view it presses in, a quick settle with a small ink-bleed, as if stamped just now. With reduced motion or no JavaScript, every stamp is already fully inked and the page reads as a finished passport.
Signature
The stamp itself. Circular customs stamps, a rectangular admittance stamp, and an oval, each carrying a date and a location. Program logos are woven in as clean travel decals beside the stamps, true-colour and never recoloured.
Palette
Kraft paper #DCC7A8 Ink #20242A Stamp red #B23A2E Passport blue #284B6F Olive #5E6B3B
Type
Spectral sets the official headings and destination names, a document serif · Hanken Grotesk carries the body · JetBrains Mono handles dates, coordinates, and entry numbers · Caveat writes the margin notes, sparingly, and never the body.
Photography
Photos render clean, taped into paper frames with washi-tape corners. The tape and paper are the only texture. No tint, blend, grain, or duotone ever touches a photograph, and nothing is upscaled past its native size.
Accessibility
Body copy is ink on kraft at about 9.5:1. Red and olive appear as text only at large sizes or inside the graphical stamps. One h1, semantic landmarks, described alt text on every photo, and visible focus rings throughout.
Choose this if
You want the warmest, most personal front door in the kit, one that treats a season as something worth keeping and makes joining feel like the start of a trip.
Think twice if
You need a dense utility homepage with standings and schedules above the fold, or a cool corporate tone. This is a keepsake, and it wants a little romance to work.

Trailhead

Ultimate grows in the understory.

In a Northwest forest, the understory is the layer where new growth happens — beneath the canopy, close to the ground, all around you. It is also a fair description of this organization. Since 1995, when a summer tournament called Potlatch grew into a nonprofit, DiscNW has held one aim above the rest: an Ultimate community without barriers.

Ten thousand players. Four seasons. Zero referees. The route below covers one full year; registration is open at every marked junction.

Est. 1995 · 10,000+ players · sliding scale from $33 · 47.68° N, 122.25° W

Waypoint 01 · Spring · April–May

Spring Reign

Every spring, the world's largest mixed youth ultimate tournament assembles on the fields at Burlington — one weekend, every age bracket, mountains on the horizon and a disc in the air at all times. Kids arrive quiet and leave hoarse. Every game ends in a spirit circle; nobody skips it.

Trail note: bring your legs.

Waypoint 02 · Summer · June–August

The Long Light

Daylight to nine o'clock and grass stains to match. Camps run all summer for ages 7–18; the Mixed Hat League drafts strangers into teams and lets a season make them friends; the Wonder League keeps the evenings interesting; and Potlatch — the tournament this whole organization grew from — still fills a holiday weekend with three generations of ultimate.

Trail note: carry water. The sunscreen is communal.

School Season

The fog comes back and so do the school buses. Middle school and high school leagues run deep into November, played in exactly the weather you'd expect and a few kinds you wouldn't. Local doctrine: there is no bad weather, only cotton.

The Quiet Season

The short days belong to footwork drills in bright gyms — and to the DiscNW Coaching Collective, where this season's sideline voices train next season's. Nothing looks like it's growing in winter. Everything is.

Rules of the trail

01

Spirit of the Game

Ultimate is self-officiated. Zero referees on any DiscNW field — players make every call, at every age, in every division. It works. It's the point.

02

No barriers

Sliding-scale fees on every program, starting at $33. Choose the tier that fits your household; every tier gets the identical season.

03

Weather

We play in rain; this is Puget Sound. When a field genuinely floods, the rainout hotline knows first: (206) 781-5840, updated by 3 p.m.

04

Leave no trace

Magnuson Park and the fields around the Sound hosted ultimate before it had a nonprofit. Pack out what you packed in, cleats and all.

Carry a layer. Leave no trace. Bring your legs.

About this direction

Nº 07 — The Understory

Thesis

A field guide to the season, told in real Northwest photography. Fog and old growth carry the atmosphere; cream cartographic panels carry the route. The year is a trail with four waypoints, and registration is open at every junction.

Signature

One dashed coral line — the disc's flight and the trail through the year — that draws itself in as you descend; photographs pinned like field specimens that develop as they arrive; the formline mark set as the trailhead medallion.

Motion

Two house curves govern everything. The hero settles like a large-format print; the trail draws in beneath your scroll; waypoint pins drop; specimens develop. With reduced motion or without JavaScript, the page is simply already there.

Station 01 · The Pull — own end zone · sunset 21:09

Every point starts the same: someone throws, everyone chases.

DiscNW is the nonprofit that keeps Northwest ultimate in the air — ten thousand players a year, Magnuson Park to the Puget Sound, every game self-officiated since 1995. This page plays out a single point, pull to catch, across one Seattle evening. The disc is already up.

Station 02 · The Possession — working the field

No hero throws.You work it up, pass by pass.

A possession is just completions strung together — a swing, a reset, a gainer. Eight programs, eight ways DiscNW moves you down the field. Every one of them is a real catch someone makes this year.

Pass 01The Backhand

Potlatch

The original. The mixed tournament that founded DiscNW in 1995 — thirty-one summers later, it's still the party the whole region circles on the calendar.

Pass 02The Swing

Keep it moving. The unhurried league of a Seattle summer — show up after work, swing it around, stay for the late light.

Pass 04The Gainer

Real yards. Camps for ages 7–18 where the first forehand of somebody's life happens before lunch, every single day.

Pass 05The Break

Pass 07The Greatest

Spring Reign

The save that shouldn't be possible. The world's largest mixed youth ultimate tournament, played in the shadow of the Cascades every spring.

Pass 08The Assist

DiscNW Coaching Collective

The throw that makes the goal. Training and mentorship that puts a real coach on every Northwest sideline that wants one.

Station 03 · The Turn — turnover, still dusk

You'll drop one. Everyone does. The next point starts anyway.

That's the honest sales pitch. Nobody here was born throwing a flick, there are no tryouts for a hat league, and there are no referees to impress — players call their own game and shake hands after. If the fee is the barrier, it slides: you choose what you pay.

Sliding scale

League fees from $33. Pick the tier that fits your year. Nobody checks, nobody asks.

No experience needed

Hat leagues draft everyone a team. Cleats help; a willingness to run helps more.

Spirit of the Game

Self-officiated, zero referees. The game has run on player calls since 1995. It holds.

About this direction

06 — One Point

The page is a single point of ultimate, narrated pull to catch: one ember flight line draws itself down the whole story as you scroll, while the evening deepens from dusk to field-light navy around it. The formline crest rides the corner like a network bug, calling each station until the disc is caught.

Palette

Type

Big Shoulders Display for titles and numerals — stadium-lettering condensed, set tight and tall. Schibsted Grotesk for narration. Spline Sans Mono for the broadcast chyrons.

Signature

A page-length SVG flight line that draws with your scroll and rides a small disc with a little real drag: it weaves behind every panel, stumbles at the turnover, vanishes into the photographs, and lands on the registration call, disc and all. In the corner, a broadcast bug carries the formline crest at 44px and calls every station, flipping to Caught — game on when the line lands. With motion off, or in print, the route shows fully flown, disc waiting at the catch.

Motion

One choreography on two house curves — a fast snap (cubic-bezier(.4,0,.1,1)) for micro moves, a long settle (.16,1,.3,1) for reveals. Station headlines rise line-by-line as they arrive; the disc chases your scroll with a little lag; the sun glow eases down as you leave the pull; the bug label rolls between stations; and over the register buttons the cursor becomes a small disc that says catch it. Scrolling stays normal everywhere. The resting state is the finished frame.

Choose this if

You want a front door people remember the next morning — one that treats a rec-league point like a broadcast moment, and makes registering feel like catching the goal.

Think twice if

You need workhorse information architecture first. This is a narrative cover, not a schedule tool — standings, rosters, and rainout utilities still need one of the grounded systems behind it.

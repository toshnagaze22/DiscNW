Fifteen points. Fifteen true things about Ultimate in the Northwest. DiscNW pulls; you receive. Every point you read is a point you score.

Point 01 · The vision0 — 1
01

An Ultimate community without barriers.

That is DiscNW’s entire vision, in six words. No gate at the field, no fine print under the mission. The next fourteen points are what those six words cost, and what they buy.

Point 02 · The scale0 — 2
02

Ten thousand players. And counting.

More than 10,000 people play with DiscNW every year, from Magnuson Park to grass fields all around Puget Sound. Less a league, more a small city that sprints.

Point 03 · The rules0 — 3
03

Zero referees. Since the first pull.

Every DiscNW game is self-officiated: players make the calls, players resolve the disputes. Spirit of the Game is not a banner slogan — it is the entire officiating system, and it has held for three decades.

Point 04 · The origin0 — 4
04

Born at Potlatch, 1995.

A tournament needed an organization, so the players built one. DiscNW grew out of the Potlatch tournament in 1995 and has been organizing the region’s Ultimate ever since.

Point 05 · The span0 — 5
05

Ages 7 to 70-plus.

Summer Youth Ultimate Camps start at seven. Masters hat leagues never really stop. Same disc, same field lines — one game, played for a whole lifetime.

Point 06 · The flagship0 — 6
06

The largest mixed youth tournament in the world.

It is called Spring Reign, and it happens here every spring. Not the region’s biggest, not the country’s — the world’s. Middle schoolers headline it.

Point 07 · The fee0 — 7
07

From $33. Sliding scale. No questions.

Registration slides down to $33 so that money never keeps anyone off the field. You pay what works; nobody checks. Here, barrier-free is a budget line, not a value statement.

Point 09 · The season0 — 9
09

Fall belongs to the schools.

Every autumn, the Middle School and High School leagues turn hallways into rosters across the region. For a lot of kids, sixth grade is where the first pull happens.

Point 10 · The hotline0 — 10
10

(206)
781‑5840

The rainout hotline. Before apps, before push alerts, Seattle Ultimate checked the fields by phone — and this number still answers. Some institutions are ten digits long.

Point 11 · The beach0 — 11
11

Washington’s only beach tournament.

Sea Plastic: sand instead of grass, salt air instead of drizzle, and the one weekend a year the state’s Ultimate takes its cleats off.

Point 12 · The coaches0 — 12
12

Coaches, coached.

The DiscNW Coaching Collective trains the people who teach the game, school team and club alike — so a kid’s first coach is a coached coach.

Point 13 · The engine0 — 13
13

Run on volunteers.

Field-liners, score reporters, tournament directors, board members. The organization runs on people who show up early, stay late, and take a shift because somebody once took one for them.

Point 14 · The roster spot0 — 14
14

You.

Fourteen points in, one thing missing. Look at the scoreboard — notice who has scored every point on this page. That is the whole model: DiscNW pulls. You score.

08 — Game to 15

The site is a game of Ultimate played to 15: a fixed scoreboard scores your reading as fifteen colossal numbered truths carry you from the first pull to a full-screen game point — the whole personality lives in the typesetting.

Signature

Choose this if
You want the page a jury retells in one sentence — “the site is a game to 15” — pure typography, cheapest to build flawlessly, and the sport’s own structure doing the branding.
